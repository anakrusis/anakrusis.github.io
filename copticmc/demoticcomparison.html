Demotic Text Comparison

The DPDP font and t2i-Egyd template encode Demotic text in different ways.
DPDP (the Demotic Palaeographical Database Project) uses the existing Unicode hieroglyphs, which are the ancestors of the Demotic glyphs. Because hieroglyphs are not 1:1 equivalent with Demotic, they can be ligatured together so that what were historically multiple hieroglyphs now can form single glyphs, and numbers can be used to disambiguate when one hieroglyph or ligature has multiple Demotic forms. The font allows the stacking of glyphs.
The t2i-Egyd template on Wiktionary, which was created by Vorziblix, constructs Demotic text out of images when given either transliterations or their Gardiner's numbers. Similar to the DPDP font, numbers afterward differentiate between variants. The ligatures and stacking of glyphs must be baked into the images. This one is more limited as it doesn't have all the commonly used glyphs and combinations, but understandable as Vorziblix has singlehandedly drawn all the images.
I am very grateful for both of their efforts toward representing Demotic text for computers, and I hope that this lays the groundwork for wider support for Demotic in the future.
