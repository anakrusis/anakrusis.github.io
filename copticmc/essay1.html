This passage consists of three cleft sentences which correspond closely with Coptic, and an aorist sentence which does not correspond straightforwardly with Coptic, but can probably be reconstructed based on the evidence that exists.

The aorist prefix ḫr

In Demotic the aorist was usually written as ḫr + (a verb in the sḏm⸗f form). A periphrastic form ḫr + (ỉr "do" in sḏm⸗f form) + (a verb in the infinitive) also existed and became increasingly common in the Roman period [c]dvs-87[/c]. It is the periphrastic form that survives in Coptic as [k]ϣⲁⲣⲉ-[/k], [k]ϣⲁ⸗[/k] [c]ce-251[/c] [c]vy-269a[/c]. The non-periphrastic ḫr + sḏm⸗f form is the one found in this passage, and it does not survive in Coptic, so the pronunciation must be reconstructed.

The main point of uncertainty here is the vowel quality of ḫr. Unstressed auxiliary verbs such as ỉr⸗f tend to be strongly reduced such that multiple vowels become one; for example Allen reconstructs /i.ri.af.'sa.ḏam/ > [k]ⲁϥⲥⲱⲧⲙ[/k] [c]aep-136[/c]. There is a possibility that the surviving Coptic forms [k]ϣⲁⲣⲉ-[/k], [k]ϣⲁ⸗[/k] may be so reduced that they provide no information on the vowel quality of ḫr.

It may be useful to compare other examples where a particle is found attached to ỉr⸗f; especially those where the non-attached form survives as well, to see how the vowel quality is affected:
• The optative[d]𓅓𓇋𓇋𓏲[/d]my ( > [k]ⲙⲁ-[/k] ) and its periphrastic form[d]𓅓𓇋𓇋𓏲𓁹:𓂋*𓏭𓇋𓏲[/d]my ỉrỉ ( > [k]ⲙⲁⲣⲉ-[/k], [k]ⲙⲁⲣⲉ⸗[/k] ) both survive in Coptic [c]ce-77, 88[/c] [c]dvs-139-142[/c]. The vowel quality of the first syllable is unchanged.
